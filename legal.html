2. What we collect and why

a) Joining the early-access waitlist

When you submit the form, we process the details you enter: your email address, your first name (optional), the programme type you are interested in, your intended intake year, and your country of residence. We also record your consent choice and, to evidence that consent under the double opt-in procedure, the date and time and IP address of your sign-up and of your email confirmation.

  • Purposes: to confirm your subscription (double opt-in); to send you the “Admissions Playbook” guide and occasional launch updates, including notifying you by email when we go live; and to understand the early-access audience in aggregate (programme, intake year, country) so we can prepare the launch.
  • Legal basis: your consent — Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR. Documenting that consent rests on our legitimate interest in being able to prove compliance — Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR.
  • Data minimisation: we deliberately do not collect nationality, ethnicity, or any special-category data (Art. 9 GDPR). Country of residence is requested instead, and only the fields above are collected.
  • Withdrawal: you can unsubscribe at any time via the link in every email or by contacting us. Withdrawal stops future processing and does not affect processing carried out before withdrawal.
  • Minimum age: you must be at least 16 to join the waitlist (the age of valid consent in Germany). The prize draw additionally requires you to be 18 or over.

b) Analytics

Only if you consent via the cookie banner, we use Plausible Analytics (cookieless, EU-hosted) to understand how the site is used in aggregate. No analytics runs before you opt in.

  • Legal basis: your consent — Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR and § 25(1) TDDDG (storing or accessing information on your device).
  • See the Cookie Notice for the specific providers and what is and is not stored.

c) Server logs

Our hosting provider processes technical access logs (e.g. IP address, timestamp, requested page, browser type) as needed to deliver and secure the site — Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR.
